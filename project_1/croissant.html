Flaky, Buttery Croissants

Ingredients

  • 2 1/4 tsp (7 g) active dry yeast
  • 1/4 cup warm water (about 43°C / 110°F)
  • 2 3/4 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/4 cup sugar
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 3/4 cup warm milk
  • 1 cup (225 g) unsalted butter, cold
  • 1 egg (for egg wash)

Instructions

  1. Activate Yeast – In a small bowl, mix yeast and warm water. Let sit for 5–10 minutes until foamy.
  2. Make Dough – In a large bowl, combine flour, sugar, and salt. Add yeast mixture and warm milk. Mix and knead until smooth. Cover and let rise for 1–1.5 hours (until doubled).
  3. Prepare Butter Layer – Place cold butter between two sheets of parchment and pound/roll into a flat rectangle. Keep chilled.
  4. Prepare Butter Layer – Place cold butter between two sheets of parchment and pound/roll into a flat rectangle. Keep chilled.
  5. Laminate Dough – Roll dough into a rectangle, place butter in the center, fold over, and seal. Roll out gently, fold into thirds (like a letter).
  6. Chill 30 minutes. Repeat rolling and folding 3 more times, chilling between folds.
  7. Shape Croissants – Roll dough into a large rectangle, cut into long triangles, and roll each from base to tip into a crescent shape.
  8. Final Rise – Place on baking sheet, cover lightly, and let rise 1–2 hours until puffy.
  9. Bake – Preheat oven to 200°C (400°F). Brush croissants with beaten egg. Bake 15–20 minutes until golden brown. Cool slightly before serving.
