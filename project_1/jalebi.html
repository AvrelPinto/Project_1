Jalebi

Ingredients

For the Batter:

  • 1 cup all-purpose flour (maida)
  • 2 tbsp cornflour
  • 1/2 tsp baking powder
  • 1/2 cup yogurt (curd)
  • 1/2 cup water (adjust as needed)
  • Pinch of turmeric or a few drops of orange food color

For the Sugar Syrup:

  • 1 cup sugar
  • 1/2 cup water
  • 1/4 tsp cardamom powder
  • Few strands saffron (optional)
  • 1/2 tsp lemon juice (prevents crystallization)

Instructions

  1. Make the batter – Mix maida, cornflour, baking powder, yogurt, and water into a smooth, thick batter. Add color. Cover and let it rest for 30–40 minutes.
  2. Prepare sugar syrup – Heat sugar and water until it reaches 1-string consistency (when a drop between two fingers forms a thin thread). Add cardamom, saffron, and lemon juice. Keep warm.
  3. Fill for piping – Pour batter into a piping bag, ketchup bottle, or cloth with a small hole.
  4. Fry jalebis – Heat ghee/oil on medium. Pipe batter directly into hot oil in spiral shapes. Fry until golden and crisp.
  5. Soak in syrup – Immediately dip fried jalebis in warm sugar syrup for 20–30 seconds, then remove.
  6. Serve hot – Best enjoyed fresh, while crisp and juicy.
